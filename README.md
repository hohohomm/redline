## You did the work. Now comes the awkward part.

The invoice is sent. A week goes by. Then two. The client hasn't replied. You open the thread three times before you write anything, delete the draft, and tell yourself you'll send it tomorrow.

Tomorrow turns into next week.

Chasing money you've already earned is the worst part of running a small business. It's awkward, it's slow, and it eats the hours you should be spending on real work.

## Redline does the chasing for you.

Send the invoice. Redline watches the inbox, follows up on the right days, and gets firmer if it slips. Friendly first, direct later, late fee applied automatically when your terms say so.

You just see who paid.

## How it works

1. **Send the invoice.** Build it in 30 seconds. Redline drops a payment link straight into the email.
2. **Rei takes over.** Your operator nudges on day 7, firms up on day 14, applies your late fee on day 21, and escalates on day 30.
3. **You see results.** One dashboard. Overdue in red, paid in warm white, what's queued in plain language.

No "just circling back." No spreadsheets. No guilt-checking your own outbox.

## Why people use it

- **Fewer awkward follow-ups.** Redline writes them. You don't.
- **Faster payment.** Clients pay sooner when reminders are consistent and arrive on time.
- **Late fees that actually happen.** Set the rule once. Redline applies it.
- **One-tap payment links.** Stripe-powered. Clients pay from the email.
- **A dashboard you'll actually open.** Built so you can read it in five seconds.

## For developers

Redline is a Next.js app on Supabase, Resend, and Stripe. Cron-driven reminder pipeline, kill-switch and dry-run baked in, audit log on every send.

```bash
npm install
cp .env.local.example .env.local   # fill in keys
npm run dev                         # http://localhost:2999
```

Stack: Next.js (App Router) · Supabase · Resend · Stripe · Vercel Cron · TypeScript · Tailwind.
